Discussion, Zoom and Journal Instructions

Discussion 1: Jesus and Discipleship

Why is it important to understand that the start of our faith, the start of our Christian life, the begin of our walk is the call of Jesus Christ? What do we risk losing if we minimize the essential role of Jesus at the start of discipleship? How can we help those we lead in the church understand that Jesus is the beginning of discipleship?

Discussions are threaded conversations interacting with one another on textbooks concepts or videos. Each discussion question is designed to help focus the conversation on various concepts being taught through the course or presented in the textbooks. Responses to the initial questions should be One-to-Two paragraphs long and should answer the question posed. Initial posts are due by midnight on Wednesday of the week the discussion is assigned.

You should interact with the responses of at least two of your course colleagues by posting by midnight on Sunday of that week. Discussion responses should reflect thoughtful interaction with the course texts and material. Each discussion will be assessed on the students' depth of analysis and response to the initial discussion question and responses to colleagues' ideas.

Discussion Post 2: Pursuing the Deeper Life

In the Christian and Missionary Alliance, we use the phrase \"deeper life\" to refer to the life lived by someone who is allowing the Spirit of God to gaze deeply into their heart and unearth sinful habits and help achieve victory over known sin and temptation.

After reading this excerpt from Alliance theologian AW Tozer, respond to the following question: Why is it essential for us to help those we lead encounter the Holy Spirit and adopt His Spiritual Field of Vision instead of our own. More practically, how can we do this effectively?

Discussion Post 3: Embracing our Identity

Dr. Roedding shared four metaphors that help us understand the essence of spiritual formation (kingdom, trellis, training, and journey) of these four which spoke to you most powerfully and why? What is the value of using metaphors or images when trying to teach men and women in your church about spiritual realities?

Discussion Post 4: The Boundaries of the Christian Life

In your own experiences, were you taught more about the exterior or interior boundary of the Christian life? Why is it important that we help people understand and respect both the interior and exterior boundaries of the Christian life?

Discussion Post 5: Practicing the Spiritual Disciplines in the Local Church 1

Describe the Spiritual Discipline you practiced in your own life last week as you completed Discovery Exercise 3. Share why it was valuable to you personally and how you feel it could be better utilized in the local church.
