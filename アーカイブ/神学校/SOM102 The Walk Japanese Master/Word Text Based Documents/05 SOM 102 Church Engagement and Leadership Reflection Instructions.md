Church Engagement Exercise Instructions

Church Engagement Exercise 1: Interview a Sr. Saint

Instructions: The objective of this exercise is to gain a better perspective on spiritual formation by spending time with someone who has followed Jesus longer than we have. Consider someone in your life who has been following Jesus longer than you have and spend time getting to know them a little better. Perhaps it's your grandmother, your Pastor, a retired Missionary, or just a friend who has a deep walk with Jesus. Schedule some time this week to connect with them in person or on the phone and ask them the following questions (by the way...feel free to ask more than just these questions).

Once you've completed this exercise, write a one-page summary of some of your key takeaways from the conversation. Questions:

1.  How do you stay connected with Jesus on a regular basis?

2.  What is your favorite passage of Scripture and why is this passage important to you?

3.  If you could go back to your "New Christian" self and give one piece of advice, what would it be and why?

4.  How have you seen your relationship with God grow over the years?

5.  What was the most difficult moment you experienced in your life and how did you experience the help of Christ during this time in your life?

Church Engagement Exercise 2: Simpson and Sanctification

The objective of this discovery exercise is two-fold. First, we want to gain an appreciation for our founders\' thoughts as they relate to Sanctification. Second, we want to better understand the interrelationship that exists between salvation and sanctification as it relates to holiness.

After reading the selected text from the book Wholly Sanctified (attached):

Develop a 2-page paper that describes your personal experience with sanctification. Using the following terms discuss your understanding of how you have experienced the separation, dedication, and filling work of God in your life. This may be written as a more experiential paper rather than an academic paper.

Develop a 1-page document that you could use as a handout in the local church that would help men and women understand the process of sanctification. Please be sure to use at least 5 passages of Scripture and at least one visual aid/element in your explanation.

You may turn these two parts of the assignment in as one document (pages 1-2 your experience with sanctification and page 3 your 1-page handout).

Church Engagement Exercise 3: Practicing the Spiritual Disciplines 1

The objective of this discovery exercise is to actually put some of the Spiritual Disciplines into practice. This week you will select one spiritual discipline (broken up by chapters) from Parts 1,2,3 or 4 in the book Spiritual Disciplines Handbook and put this discipline into practice in your own life.

After practicing this spiritual discipline throughout the week, you will be tasked with developing and sharing a one-page instructional guide that could be used by a lay-person in the local church to practice your discipline. Be creative in the way that you design the instruction guide, but it should address the what, how, and why of your discipline.

When submitting this work for grading please also post this one-page instructional guide in the discussion post marked: \"Spiritual Disciplines Guides Part 1\" which is located in this week\'s Moodle section.\* This will enable your fellow colleague to gain multiple instructional sheets for use in the local church.

Reflection Exercise 2: Identity in Christ

Write a 2-3 page reflection paper on how your identity in Christ can help you feel valued and loved. Use 3 specific Scriptures to support your conclusions.

Additionally, develop a 1-2 page handout that could be used in the local church to give Scriptural support to how an Identity in Christ could help an individual find: peace, love, acceptance, security, and significance.

Reflection Exercise 2b: Learning Reflection

This reflection will be an opportunity for you to critically evaluate various course-related topics for their theological importance, ministry leadership relevance, and personal application. It is necessary to format all reflection exercises using the MLA style.
