School of Ministry -- Self-Directed Coursework

Course 102: The Walk

**Course Description:**

The call of Christ is a call to be different. What does it look like to follow Jesus? The Walk is a course that focuses our attention on becoming a modern-day disciple, a follower of Christ who looks more like the Savior day by day. This class introduces students to discipleship, sanctification, spiritual formation, and the spiritual disciples of engagement and abstinence.

**Student Learning Objectives:**

1.  Understand the essential components of biblical discipleship.

2.  Comprehend basic elements of spiritual formation.

3.  Demonstrate the practical use of spiritual disciplines in the life of Christian and the life of the Church.

4.  Analyze the central role of the Holy Spirit in sanctification.

2.  Assignments

[Online Assignments (SLO 1,2,3,4)]{.underline}

SOM uses the website learn.leadersalliance.org for all course management. Each unit, students will receive video instructions for the required reading and videos to watch. Be sure to listen to these instructions before beginning any online work for that unit.

 

[Journals (SLO 1,2,4)]{.underline}

For each Unit, students will be required to submit journal entries. Students will respond to course questions as they complete their journal entries. Each journal should be 600-700 words in length and will be graded for content, thoroughness, and grammar.

[Church Engagement Exercises (SLO 3,4)]{.underline}

During Units 1,2,4 & 5, students will practice the skills they are studying in a particular unit. The primary goal of these assignments is to give them experience in various skills necessary for ministry leadership. These assignments do not need to be in MLA style; they are graded on their thoroughness, completion, or required elements (unique to each assignment) for each exercise and grammar. 

[Leadership Reflection Exercises (SLO -- 1,2,3)]{.underline}

> For Units 3 & 6, students will write a 3-4-page reflection on what they learned. This reflection will be an opportunity for students to critically evaluate various course-related topics for their theological importance, ministry leadership relevance, and personal application. It [is necessary]{.underline} to format all reflection exercises using the MLA style.
>
>  

[Quizzes (SLO 1,2,3,4)]{.underline}

> Quizzes are to serve as key points of review. Questions are from instructor-led material and course readings. Quizzes are open book. Therefore, students are encouraged to refer to their course lectures and reading material while taking the quiz.
>
>  

**Course Procedures**

1.  Classroom Practices

    a.  Netiquette

> Students must use communication that glorifies God. We do not tolerate name-calling and profanity; it is okay to disagree respectfully or offer constructive comments. Remember, we aim to build up rather than tear down. Speak the way you would want to be spoken to.

b.  Participation in Online Classes

> Due to the self-directed nature of this course, regular online interaction and participation are essential. The evaluator will contact students who are inactive for an extended period during the course without prior approval from the evaluator or Dean to check in.

c.  Assignment grading and response

> Most assignments will be graded and returned to students within one week of their submission. Fast, constructive feedback is vital to the online learning experience.

2.  Grading Policy and Rating Scale

    a.  The program uses the following grading scale for your final course grade:

> Excellent (92-100)
>
> Above Average (84-91)
>
> Average (76-83)
>
> Below Average (75-65)
>
> Unacceptable (0-64)

b.  The following weights will be used for this course:

> Church Engagement Exercises 20%
>
> Leadership Reflection Exercises 40%
>
> Journal Posts 25%
>
> Quizzes 15%
>
> Total: 100%
