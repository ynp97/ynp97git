School of Ministry -- Accelerated Cohort Format

Course 102: The Walk

**Course Description:**

The call of Christ is a call to be different. But what does it look like to follow Jesus? The Walk is a course that focuses our attention on becoming a modern-day disciple; a follower of Christ who, day by day, is looking more like the Savior. This class is six weeks long and introduces students to the topics of discipleship, sanctification, spiritual formation and the spiritual disciples of engagement and abstinence.

**Student Learning Objectives:**

1.  Understand the basic components of biblical discipleship.

2.  Comprehend basic elements of spiritual formation.

3.  Demonstrate the practical use of spiritual disciplines in the life of Christian and the life of the Church.

4.  Analyze the central role of the Holy Spirit in sanctification.

2.  Assignments

[Online Assignments (SLO 1,2,3,4)]{.underline}

SOM uses the website learn.allianceleaders.org for all online work. Please be sure to log-in regularly to this website to complete all course work. Furthermore, all course work must be submitted through this portal.

[Discussions (SLO 1,2,4)]{.underline}

Discussions are threaded conversations interacting with one another on textbooks concepts or videos. Each discussion question is designed to help focus the conversation on various concepts being taught through the course or presented in the textbooks. Responses to the initial questions should be One-to-Two paragraphs long and should answer the question posed. Initial posts are due by *midnight on Wednesday* of the week the discussion is assigned.

You should interact with the responses of at least two of your course colleagues by posting by *midnight on Sunday of that week*. Discussion responses should reflect thoughtful interaction with the course texts and material. Each discussion will be assessed on the students' depth of analysis and response to the initial discussion question and responses to colleagues' ideas.

[Church Engagement Exercises (SLO 3,4)]{.underline}

During Units 1,2,4 & 5 you will be asked to practice the skills that we are studying in a particular unit. The primary goal of these assignments is to give you experience in various skills necessary for practical ministry. These assignments do not need to be formatted in an MLA style unless specifically noted. They will be graded on their thoroughness, completion or required elements (unique to each assignment) for each exercise, and grammar.

[Leadership Reflection Exercises (SLO -- 1,2,3)]{.underline}

For Units 3 & 6 you will be asked to reflect on various aspects of ministry leadership. These assignments will be slightly more in-depth than the Church Engagement Exercises. These assignments will be an opportunity for you to critically evaluate various course-related topics for their theological importance, ministry leadership relevance, and personal application. These assignments must be formatted using the MSL style unless specifically noted. They will be graded on their thoroughness, completion, the inclusion of required elements, and for grammar.

[Quizzes (SLO 1,2,3,4)]{.underline}

Quizzes are meant to serve as key points of review. Questions are taken from instructor-led material and course readings. Quizzes are open-book meaning students are encouraged to refer to their course lectures and reading material when taking each quiz.

**Course Procedures**

1.  Classroom Practices

    a.  Netiquette

> Students are expected to use communication that glorifies God. Name calling, or profanity are not tolerated; it is okay to disagree respectfully or offer constructive comments. Remember, we aim to build up rather than tear down. Speak the way you would want to be spoken to.

b.  Participation in Online Classes

> Due to the accelerated nature of this course, regular online interaction and participation are essential. Students who are inactive for a week during the course without prior approval from the evaluator or Dean will be contacted by the evaluator in order to check in.

c.  Assignment grading and response

> Most assignments will be graded and returned to students within one week of their submission. Fast, constructive feedback is vital to the online learning experience.

2.  Grading Policy and Rating Scale

    a.  The program uses the following grading scale for your final course grade:

> A (Excellent) = (92-100)
>
> B (Above Average)= (84-91)
>
> C (Average) = (76-83)
>
> D (Below Average) = (75-65)
>
> F = (0-64)

b.  The following weights will be used for this course:

    i.  Church Engagement Exercises 20%

    ii. Leadership Reflection Exercises 40%

    iii. Discussion Posts 25%

    iv. Quizzes 15%

> Total: 100%

3.  Late Assignment Policy

Late work will be accepted, but with a one category deduction. For instance, if a student turns in a reflection paper that meets the Excellent standard, but the paper is late, that paper would receive an Above Average grade. Evaluators are willing to work with students who have difficult circumstances, but a sense of fairness to the rest of the class must be maintained.
