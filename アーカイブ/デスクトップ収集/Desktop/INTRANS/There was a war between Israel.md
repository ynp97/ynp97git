There was a war between Israel, God's people, and a country called Philistia,

which made fun of God.

In that war, the two sides decided to choose one man from each side

and let them fight to decide the winner.

From the Philistines came a huge man named Goliath.(ゴライアス） He was about three meters tall.

The soldiers of Israel were normal-sized men, so they thought,

"We cannot win." No one could say, "I will fight him."

But then a boy named David came there.

He had only come to bring lunch to his older brothers, who were soldiers.

Even though his brothers were very afraid, David said, "I will fight," and he went out.

Then, with just one stone that he picked up, he knocked Goliath down and saved Israel.

＠＠

What kind of boy was David?

David was a boy who listened well to God.

He was the kind of child who helped at home and praised God all the time.

Then, when war came, that child defeated the strongest enemy, the one all the grown-ups were afraid of.

That is the story of David and Goliath.

＠＠

Why did David win?

It was because God was with David.

God loved David and was always with him, so David was able to do something great.

＠＠

Then how did God give David strength?

What David did was this: he picked up one stone and threw it.

The stone hit the other man on the head, and he fell down.

Some people may think that God did a miracle and gave strength to weak young David,

and that something amazing happened only because of a miracle.

But when we read the Bible carefully, we can see that this is not the whole story.

We can see that David already had very great strength at that time.

The Bible says that David had fought wild animals like lions and bears.

So this means that David had already been given the strength to fight a giant. This is the important point.

1 Sam. 17:34  David said to Saul, "Your servant used to keep sheep for his father. And when there came a lion, or a bear, and took a lamb from the flock,

1 Sam. 17:35  I went after him and struck him and delivered it out of his mouth. And if he arose against me, I caught him by his beard and struck him and killed him."

A lion can run as fast as 80 kilometers per hour, like a car. That is very fast.

A bear is very strong. It can be about twice as strong as a grown man. It also has very sharp claws.

But even against such strong animals, David had won.

1 Sam. 17:49  And David put his hand in his bag and took out a stone and slung it and struck the Philistine on his forehead. The stone sank into his forehead, and he fell on his face to the ground.

1 Sam. 17:50  So David prevailed over the Philistine with a sling and with a stone, and struck the Philistine and killed him. There was no sword in the hand of David.

The tool he used was called a sling.

It was a weapon that could send a stone flying far away---whoosh! It was used at the front line in battle.

So David had learned very well how to use that powerful weapon.

He won by using what had already been given to him.

＠＠＠＠

When something hard happens to us,

we should try to look again at what has already been given to us.

To find that, prayer is very important.

When we keep talking with God, it becomes easier to see.

And in that prayer, before we say, "I do not have enough," or, "This is too hard,"

we should first thank God for what we already have.

Yes, life can be hard. But God does not make mistakes.

When we pray with thanks, even in hard times, we will begin to see the way we should go.

@@@@@

There is one more important point in this story.

When David was going to fight, the king said, "Take this armor and this sword." These were the things everyone else used.

But when David put them on, he lost the speed and accuracy of the gift God had given him.

David knew his own gift well, so he said no to the king's offer.

He fought in his own way and won.

@@@

That means he did not follow the power of this world, shown by the king.

He followed God's way.

@@

So what should we learn from this?

A time may come when we also have to fight.

But we should remember that our way of fighting may be different from the way the world fights.

People may try to become strong in ways everyone can see.

They may try to get more money or a better title, and use those things to win against others.

But in a battle with the Lord, those things may not be real weapons.

Sometimes they may even get in the way.

Then what kind of weapon do we need?

We need the kind of weapon that helps us not crush other people, but love them.

If we accept others, love others, and let go of our own rights, then we can have true victory.

And for us Christians, God has already given us the gift of loving people.

So let us find that gift well, and in our daily life, throw away hatred and jealousy, and fight with love.
