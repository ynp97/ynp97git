\*\*Luke 24:1-12 \"He Is Not Among the Dead\"\*\*

Today, let\'s read the account of the resurrection.

\*\*1 Corinthians 15:3\*\* - \"For what I received I passed on to you as of first importance: that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures,

\*\*1 Corinthians 15:4\*\* - that He was buried, that He was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures,

\*\*1 Corinthians 15:5\*\* - and that He appeared to Cephas, and then to the Twelve.

\*\*1 Corinthians 15:6\*\* - After that, He appeared to more than five hundred of the brothers and sisters at the same time, most of whom are still living, though some have fallen asleep.

\*\*1 Corinthians 15:7\*\* - Then He appeared to James, then to all the apostles,

\*\*1 Corinthians 15:8\*\* - and last of all He appeared to me also, as to one abnormally born.\"

As Paul teaches, the resurrection is of utmost importance.

However, as you may know, it is also true that some people hesitate to take a step of faith in Jesus because they find it hard to believe in the resurrection.

Today, let\'s delve into this difficult yet crucial aspect of faith---the resurrection.

In the previous chapter, we read about Jesus\' burial. Joseph\'s lavish burial arrangement is quite striking. Under normal circumstances, it might have been expected that Jesus\' body would be discarded. But instead, a truly grand, heartfelt burial took place. The cost of this was likely significant, and this is clearly described in the text.

We can see this as the end of Jesus\' humble journey through the cross, with the beginning of His glorification. He was received as the true King. The climax of this is the miraculous resurrection.

\*\*Luke 24:1\*\* - \"On the first day of the week, very early in the morning, the women took the spices they had prepared and went to the tomb.

\*\*Luke 24:2\*\* - They found the stone rolled away from the tomb,

\*\*Luke 24:3\*\* - but when they entered, they did not find the body of the Lord Jesus.

\*\*Luke 24:4\*\* - While they were wondering about this, suddenly two men in clothes that gleamed like lightning stood beside them.\"

\*\*Matthew 28:1\*\* - \"After the Sabbath, at dawn on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary went to look at the tomb.

\*\*Matthew 28:2\*\* - There was a violent earthquake, for an angel of the Lord came down from heaven and, going to the tomb, rolled back the stone and sat on it.

\*\*Matthew 28:3\*\* - His appearance was like lightning, and his clothes were white as snow.

\*\*Matthew 28:4\*\* - The guards were so afraid of him that they shook and became like dead men.\"

The way this scene is described differs between the Gospels.

In Matthew, the earthquake is mentioned immediately following Jesus\' death.

Regarding the scene of the resurrection, there are differences in the details, such as what the angels were like and where they were located.

There are various theories about these discrepancies, but my personal opinion is that when heavenly events take place on earth, they surpass human perception.

The differences in testimony might not be contradictions, but rather reflections of the fact that what was actually happening was beyond the witnesses\' comprehension. In other words, all accounts are true from the perspective of those who witnessed them.

\*\*Luke 24:5\*\* - \"In their fright the women bowed down with their faces to the ground, but the men said to them, \'Why do you look for the living among the dead?

\*\*Luke 24:6\*\* - He is not here; He has risen! Remember how He told you, while He was still with you in Galilee:

\*\*Luke 24:7\*\* - The Son of Man must be delivered over to the hands of sinners, be crucified and on the third day be raised again.\'

\*\*Luke 24:8\*\* - Then they remembered His words.\"

Angels appear at key moments in Jesus\' life. The first time was at His birth.

The second was after His testing in the wilderness when angels ministered to Him.

The next appearance is during His prayer in Gethsemane.

Then, at His resurrection, and finally at His ascension, angels appeared to the disciples.

They play important roles at each of these pivotal moments.

The angels pointed out that the women were looking for Jesus\' body, meaning they had not yet realized the significance of the resurrection.

Even though they had been near the tomb in the previous chapter, they were not waiting for the resurrection but were staying out of love for Jesus, reluctant to part from Him.

Now, these women were confronted not with a body but with an empty tomb.

Why did God show the people the empty tomb? One major reason was to have them confirm that the body was no longer there.

This was to demonstrate that Jesus' resurrection was not just a spiritual or ghostly resurrection, but a bodily one.

The sealing of the tomb and the opening of it in front of the people were essential steps to show that the resurrection was real.

\*\*Luke 24:9\*\* - \"When they came back from the tomb, they told all these things to the Eleven and to all the others.\"

What did they report?

\*\*Matthew 28:7\*\* - \"Then go quickly and tell His disciples: 'He has risen from the dead and is going ahead of you into Galilee. There you will see Him.' Now I have told you.\"

This had already been foretold.

\*\*Matthew 26:32\*\* - \"But after I have risen, I will go ahead of you into Galilee.\"

Why Galilee? This signifies a new beginning in His ministry.

\*\*Matthew 4:12\*\* - \"When Jesus heard that John had been put in prison, He withdrew to Galilee.

\*\*Matthew 4:13\*\* - Leaving Nazareth, He went and lived in Capernaum, which was by the lake in the area of Zebulun and Naphtali---

\*\*Matthew 4:14\*\* - to fulfill what was said through the prophet Isaiah:

\*\*Matthew 4:15\*\* - \'Land of Zebulun and land of Naphtali, the Way of the Sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the Gentiles---

\*\*Matthew 4:16\*\* - the people living in darkness have seen a great light; on those living in the land of the shadow of death a light has dawned.\'

\*\*Matthew 4:17\*\* - From that time on Jesus began to preach, \'Repent, for the kingdom of heaven has come near.\'\"

A new phase of ministry was beginning, but this time it was not focused on the chosen people of Israel.

It was a mission to all people walking in darkness, to all of humanity.

\*\*Matthew 28:8\*\* - \"So the women hurried away from the tomb, afraid yet filled with joy, and ran to tell His disciples.

\*\*Matthew 28:9\*\* - Suddenly Jesus met them. \'Greetings,\' He said. They came to Him, clasped His feet and worshiped Him.

\*\*Matthew 28:10\*\* - Then Jesus said to them, \'Do not be afraid. Go and tell my brothers to go to Galilee; there they will see Me.\'\"

Mark includes an additional detail:

\*\*Mark 16:8\*\* - \"Trembling and bewildered, the women went out and fled from the tomb. They said nothing to anyone, because they were afraid.\"

At first, they were in this state. However, upon further reflection, they obediently followed the angel\'s words.

At that moment, Jesus appeared before their eyes, allowing them to see Him clearly.

He spoke to them, and they were able to touch His feet. How overjoyed they must have been!

With this joy, they followed the instructions they were given and ran to tell the disciples.

Here, the fact that they saw, heard, and touched Jesus is significant.

By experiencing the resurrected Jesus through their senses---touch, sight, and hearing---they were able to accept a reality that seemed impossible.

This experience empowered them to gather the disciples with stronger conviction.

It was only because they overcame their fear and took a step of faith that they were able to have this experience.

\*\*Luke 24:10\*\* - \"It was Mary Magdalene, Joanna, Mary the mother of James, and the others with them who told this to the apostles.

\*\*Luke 24:11\*\* - But they did not believe the women, because their words seemed to them like nonsense.\"

But the disciples did not believe. How astonishing!

Moreover, throughout the events from the crucifixion to the resurrection, the disciples were nowhere to be seen.

In contrast, we see that the women were always nearby.

Did these women who appear in the story have any expectation of the resurrection?

In verse 8, we see that the angels reminded them, and they remembered the words about the resurrection. So they were not unaware that Jesus would rise on the third day.

However, the fact that they brought oils and spices to treat the body shows that they were not anticipating the resurrection.

If they had been sure of the resurrection, they would have brought something like porridge instead.

Even when they saw the empty tomb, the women did not immediately feel the joy of the resurrection.

The empty tomb and the prophecy of the resurrection had not yet connected in their minds.

As written in Luke, they had forgotten. Why had they forgotten?

To put it bluntly, it was because they had not fully trusted in God.

Although they had believed in Jesus as a man---someone they could see, touch, and hear---they had not yet fully believed in Him as God.

They believed in \"Jesus the man,\" not \"Jesus the God.\"

These women had witnessed many miracles, including the resurrection of Lazarus. They knew that resurrection was something that could happen in reality.

Furthermore, both the women and the disciples had often heard Jesus teach about the resurrection and the faith in God that brings it about.

So they understood it intellectually.

But perhaps, deep down, they thought that miracles only happened because Jesus was physically present. Without Him, miracles could not occur, because they had seen Jesus, but they had never seen God.

Such feelings likely grew stronger in the face of Jesus\' death---a seemingly hopeless situation.

Peter, Mary, and the others were, in a sense, dependent on the visible, human Jesus.

They were in a state of dependence on Him, as though they could not look beyond the Son of God to see God Himself.

It is understandable, given how remarkable Jesus was, that they would fix their eyes on Him as a person.

But this was not the perspective that God desired.

Jesus\' death and resurrection were necessary to direct our gaze toward the true God.

Jesus was the sacrificial offering necessary to save us from our sins. To accomplish this, He needed to become human.

In the midst of human relationships, Jesus entrusted His disciples with teachings that would restore the lost bond between God and humanity.

And in order to instill the faith to believe in the unseen, Jesus first provided the disciples with a visible, tangible impact, which was later taken away.

The disciples were ordinary people. Peter, for instance, is often depicted sleeping, crying, or getting angry---hardly a shining example in the Scriptures.

In Mark 14:51, it mentions a young man who followed Jesus while wearing nothing but a linen garment, but he fled naked when seized. Some believe this young man was Mark himself. In any case, everyone was painfully ordinary.

It was necessary for Jesus Christ to die and rise again to give such people the \"realization\" they needed.

God knew that human beings are weak and that believing in the unseen is difficult. That is why He sent the Son of Man---someone they could see and touch---so that they could eventually be able to believe in the unseen.

\*\*Luke 24:12\*\* - \"Peter, however, got up and ran to the tomb. Bending over, he saw the strips of linen lying by themselves, and he went away, wondering to himself what had happened.\"

Jesus left behind the \"empty tomb\" and disappeared from that place. As a result, the \"empty tomb\" and the \"linen cloths\" became visible, historical facts for all people, regardless of their perspective.

Regarding the linen cloths: It appears that the body was wrapped in thin, white strips of cloth, much like bandages. It is said that the cloth around the head was a bit wider. In other words, Jesus was not wrapped like a mummy.

Let us recall a particular passage:

\*\*John 11:38\*\* - \"Jesus, once more deeply moved, came to the tomb. It was a cave with a stone laid across the entrance.

\*\*John 11:39\*\* - \'Take away the stone,\' He said. \'But, Lord,\' said Martha, the sister of the dead man, \'by this time there is a bad odor, for he has been there four days.\'

\*\*John 11:40\*\* - Then Jesus said, \'Did I not tell you that if you believe, you will see the glory of God?\'

\*\*John 11:41\*\* - So they took away the stone. Then Jesus looked up and said, \'Father, I thank You that You have heard Me.

\*\*John 11:42\*\* - I knew that You always hear Me, but I said this for the benefit of the people standing here, that they may believe that You sent Me.\'

\*\*John 11:43\*\* - When He had said this, Jesus called in a loud voice, \'Lazarus, come out!\'

\*\*John 11:44\*\* - The dead man came out, his hands and feet wrapped with strips of linen, and a cloth around his face. Jesus said to them, \'Take off the grave clothes and let him go.\'\"

This is the passage about the revival of Lazarus. Jesus\' resurrection was different from the revival of Lazarus. Lazarus came out still wrapped in long strips of cloth. This might give the image of a mummy walking. However, Jesus' resurrection was different. The long cloths were left behind in the tomb. Even the cloth that had been wrapped around His head remained. Lazarus\' revival was a return to life that would eventually lead to death again. But Jesus\' resurrection was a victory over death.

What happened inside the empty tomb was not a revival but a resurrection. It wasn't merely a delay of death but a triumph over it. We must carefully observe this contrast.

The miracle of the resurrection presents us with a challenge. Can we believe in Jesus, whom we can no longer see with our eyes? Can we, like the disciples who received the Holy Spirit and eventually faced martyrdom, grasp unwavering faith? The disciples faced this challenge as well, and as we can see from their actions later, it wasn't an easy one.

In summary, through the resurrection, we are freed from believing only in what we can see. We transition to believing in what we cannot see. This step of faith---believing in the unseen---is supported by God, and He builds a new foundation of faith through our experiences.

This \"challenge of the empty tomb\" also reveals our human \"weakness of faith.\" When we encounter the miracle of the resurrection, we realize the weakness of our own faith.

\*\*Mark 16:8\*\* - \"Trembling and bewildered, the women went out and fled from the tomb. They said nothing to anyone, because they were afraid.\"

Mary and the others trembled, panicked, and fled. They were terrified.

This fear arose from their realization of God\'s righteousness.

I believe they grasped the significance of the empty tomb at that moment. They realized who it was they had been spending time with. Reflecting on Jesus\' words and deeds, they saw that everything had been leading up to this single point---the resurrection. This realization must have shaken them deeply.

If that was the case, they became aware of their previous weakness of faith. And they feared before God.

History tells us that they overcame their fear and eventually shared the news with the disciples. How did they change when they recovered from their temporary fear?

They remembered.

They remembered that what Jesus had spoken about in His ministry was not abstract or symbolic, but something that would indeed happen.

What seemed impossible, what seemed unimaginable, had become possible and real through the miracle of the resurrection. If that was true, then the things Jesus had spoken about---eternal life, God\'s love for us, and all His abundant blessings---were also true. They were given this certainty.

The empty tomb challenges our faith. Can you believe in the unseen Jesus Christ? This challenge touches on our weakness of faith, but in the end, it strengthens us.

When we believe in the unseen Jesus, and when our faith grows stronger, like Mary, we become convinced that the promises of Jesus in the Bible are real.

The compassion and love of Jesus described in the Scriptures are truly given to each of us as grace. Even if we are now in unbearable suffering, it will surely be healed in the eternal time we spend with Jesus.

Sorrow and suffering will one day be transformed into joy and peace in the eternal life we will spend with God. When we believe in Jesus and are assured of this grace, our lives are changed.

Let's remember the basic teachings of Jesus once more: We cannot deal with our sins by our own strength. But becoming a Christian means being reborn from that state. That is why we can draw near to Jesus despite our deeply rooted sins.

Upon hearing this, we might wonder: Is such a thing really possible? We may be able to hide the ugliness deep in our hearts, but can we truly get rid of it?

However, the challenge of the resurrection miracle provides the answer. We are able to walk this path. We can move forward on what seems like an impossible road---drawing nearer to the image of God.

The miracle of the resurrection guarantees that we can live as new creations, reborn through Jesus Christ.

And the Almighty Lord, Jesus Christ, is always with us, holding a perfect plan for each one of us. We can trust in this. In other words, everything will be okay. It's alright to feel secure. This belief, which the world may mock, is also a \"sign\" that allows us to trust wholeheartedly.

For those who still find it difficult to believe, this passage offers a hint. Like Mary, try to stay as close to Jesus as possible---not because you fully believe or understand, but because of the sympathy you feel for Him in your heart. Cherish that feeling. This might mean attending worship services, even if you don't fully believe yet. It might mean reading the Bible, even if you're not certain yet.

As you take small steps closer, the Lord will surely give you insight. He has already prepared something that will lead you to believe in what you cannot see. It might be a miracle that surprises you, or it might be a quiet, persistent feeling in your heart---a desire to continue your relationship with Jesus, even though you don't fully understand.

To catch such moments, you need to stay close to Jesus. If you're too far away, a miracle may seem like mere coincidence or luck, and the stirrings in your heart may feel like fleeting thoughts. Like Peter, being distant may lead to difficulty in believing.

But when you spend time near the Lord, like Mary and the others, you will come to realize that this is indeed the work of God.

Believing is not easy. The very word \"believe\" implies that there are things we do not fully understand. The object of faith cannot be fully comprehended, and that is the foundation of belief.

However, we must not forget that this is also why faith can be strong. Faith doesn't require explanation or convincing others because God transcends human understanding. It is precisely because we cannot explain God that He is God.

Because we cannot comprehend it, we must believe. And when we believe, we receive God\'s peace.

The God we believe in transcends all human understanding, yet He knows human death and suffering. The Lord does not call us to practice some extreme form of enlightenment or transcend everything through intense training. He is not some distant cosmic force that simply exists out there. He is a personal God who loves each and every one of us.

While we cannot prove this, we have already experienced glimpses of this great, loving God in our lives. Though we cannot explain it, our hearts have already been changed.

Let us nurture this growing faith. The Lord will surely bless this process. Let us rejoice in the new life we live with the Lord, who is no longer among the dead.
