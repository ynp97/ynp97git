**September 15, 2024 Luke 23:49--56 "What They Realized When They Buried Jesus"**

Today\'s passage is the scene of the burial that lies between the death on the cross and the resurrection.

\*\*Luke 23:50\*\*

\"Now there was a man named Joseph, from the Jewish town of Arimathea. He was a member of the council, a good and righteous man,\"

\*\*Luke 23:51\*\*

\"who had not consented to their decision and action; and he was looking for the kingdom of God.\"

\-\--

\*\*Matthew 27:57\*\*

\"When it was evening, there came a rich man from Arimathea, named Joseph, who also was a disciple of Jesus.\"

\-\--

Arimathea is a town between Jerusalem and Samaria. He was a wealthy man and a council member. The mention of him being rich is found only in Matthew. From the fact that he owned a new tomb, it appears he had moved to Jerusalem from the countryside. He was not a disciple who had followed from rural areas like Galilee.

It is written that he was a council member, so he was involved with the Sanhedrin.

\*\*Why did he not oppose at the time of the trial?\*\*

1\. \*\*Because he felt fear or danger:\*\*

According to \*\*John 19:38\*\*, Joseph was \"a disciple of Jesus, but secretly for fear of the Jews.\" By opposing openly, he might have exposed himself or his family to the danger of persecution, so he may have refrained from speaking out.

2\. \*\*Because he was in the minority in the council:\*\*

\*\*Luke 23:51\*\* states that \"he had not consented to their decision and action.\" In other words, although he held an opposing view, the majority of the Sanhedrin (Jewish council) had decided on Jesus\' execution, and it might have been impossible for him alone to overturn that decision.

3\. \*\*He might not have made it in time for the council\'s decision:\*\*

Jesus\' trial was hastily conducted at night (\*\*Mark 14:53--65\*\*). Therefore, it\'s possible that Joseph was not present and had no opportunity to oppose.

However, ultimately, in response to Jesus\' death, he took the most courageous step forward, so much so that he even came to be associated with various legends.

\*\*Luke 23:52\*\*

\"This man went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus.\"

\*\*Luke 23:53\*\*

\"Then he took it down and wrapped it in a linen shroud and laid him in a tomb cut in stone, where no one had ever yet been laid.\"

\-\--

\*\*Matthew 27:58\*\*

\"He went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus. Then Pilate ordered it to be given to him.

\*\*Matthew 27:59\*\*

\"And Joseph took the body and wrapped it in a clean linen shroud\"

\*\*Matthew 27:60\*\*

\"and laid it in his own new tomb, which he had cut in the rock. And he rolled a great stone to the entrance of the tomb and went away.\"

\-\--

Now, regarding taking down from the cross and requesting the body, in relation to \*\*Deuteronomy 21:22--23\*\*, and considering the timing of the coming Sabbath, this was the only time to do it.

\-\--

\*\*Deuteronomy 21:22\*\*

\"And if a man has committed a crime punishable by death and he is put to death, and you hang him on a tree,\"

\*\*Deuteronomy 21:23\*\*

\"his body shall not remain all night on the tree, but you shall bury him the same day, for a hanged man is cursed by God. You shall not defile your land that the LORD your God is giving you for an inheritance.\"

\-\--

\*\*Next, let\'s discuss the method of burial, starting with some additional information.\*\*

\-\--

\*\*John 19:38\*\*

\"After these things Joseph of Arimathea, who was a disciple of Jesus, but secretly for fear of the Jews, asked Pilate that he might take away the body of Jesus. Pilate gave him permission, so he came and took away his body.\"

\*\*John 19:39\*\*

\"Nicodemus also, who earlier had come to Jesus by night, came bringing a mixture of myrrh and aloes, about seventy-five pounds in weight.\"

\*\*John 19:40\*\*

\"So they took the body of Jesus and bound it in linen cloths with the spices, as is the burial custom of the Jews.\"

\*\*John 19:41\*\*

\"Now in the place where he was crucified there was a garden, and in the garden a new tomb in which no one had yet been laid.\"

\*\*John 19:42\*\*

\"So because of the Jewish day of Preparation, since the tomb was close at hand, they laid Jesus there.\"

\-\--

Nicodemus also stepped forward and seemed to have brought many valuable items.

The burial method involved wrapping long strips of cloth around the body and inserting spices into the folds. This was precisely the way kings were buried. The spices were said to be extremely expensive. They brought about 30 kilograms (approximately 75 pounds), which is quite a large amount. The tomb was brand new; ordinarily, tombs were used by multiple people.

What is notable about Jesus\'s burial is that it was done very carefully and lavishly. The clean linen cloth was naturally precious, and the new tomb was of great value. By bringing these items together, they revealed Jesus to the world as a king. This didn\'t happen by chance but was a fulfillment of Scripture---the prophecy about Jesus in Isaiah 53.

\-\--

\*\*Isaiah 53:8\*\*

\"By oppression and judgment he was taken away;

and as for his generation, who considered

that he was cut off out of the land of the living,

stricken for the transgression of my people?\"

\*\*Isaiah 53:9\*\*

\"And they made his grave with the wicked

and with a rich man in his death,

although he had done no violence,

and there was no deceit in his mouth.\"

\-\--

Only Matthew records that Joseph was a rich man. Since Matthew emphasizes the fulfillment of Scripture, he likely wrote this to highlight the part \"with a rich man.\"

Everything related to Jesus\'s crucifixion can be seen in this light. Through this burial, what might look to the world like the execution of a miserable, failed messiah is proven to have been carried out under Jesus\'s sovereignty, with authority, as the true King.

The Jesus who had been proven to be King by enduring beating and insults---contrary to expectations---is now beginning to be revealed in the way He should be.

\*\*So, who conducted this burial? Two individuals:\*\*

\-\--

\*\*Joseph\*\*

All the Gospels record that a man named Joseph of Arimathea asked Pilate for Jesus\'s body and buried it. Descriptions of him vary among the Gospels:

\- \*\*Matthew\*\* calls him \"a rich man and a disciple of Jesus.\"

\- \*\*Mark\*\* describes him as \"a respected member of the council.\"

\- \*\*Luke\*\* says he was \"a good and righteous man\" who was \"looking for the kingdom of God.\"

\- \*\*John\*\* portrays him as \"a disciple of Jesus, but secretly for fear of the Jews.\"

\-\--

\*\*Nicodemus\*\*

Mentioned in the Gospel of John, Nicodemus was a Pharisee and a member of the Sanhedrin. He respected Jesus and visited Him secretly at night to have a dialogue (John 3:1--21). When Jewish leaders criticized Jesus, Nicodemus defended Him by saying, \"Does our law judge a man without first giving him a hearing and learning what he does?\"

\-\--

\*\*What do these two have in common?\*\*

They both believed in Jesus but were unable to openly express it. Joseph was \"a disciple of Jesus, but secretly for fear of the Jews,\" and Nicodemus was someone who visited Jesus secretly at night for discussions.

\*\*So what did they do?\*\*

They honored Jesus openly in front of the people. There must have been many Jews present. Since they had status and honor, they were likely well-known individuals. Acquaintances might have been nearby. Despite such circumstances, as we saw earlier, they gave Jesus the highest honor in burial with great respect.

\-\--

\*\*Let\'s organize the situation.\*\*

From the world\'s perspective at that time, Jesus was merely a failed messiah. The crowds had lost their initial enthusiasm and thought, \"He did amazing things, but this man Jesus died so easily. We were almost deceived.\"

In other words, giving Jesus a proper burial wouldn\'t earn them any appreciation from others. People would simply think, \"Oh! I suspected those guys were suspicious, but they were supporters of that man Jesus! They\'re heretics against Judaism!\" Such a stigma was a significant blow in that society. That\'s why they had kept their faith hidden until now.

In fact, the disciples who were close to Jesus---those twelve who proudly declared, \"I am a disciple of this amazing person!\" when Jesus was popular---did not appear here. Being a disciple of Jesus was that dangerous at this time.

By burying Jesus, they would be ostracized from society. Their act of burying Jesus placed them in such adversity. In other words, it could be considered a suicidal act of throwing away their wealth and honor.

However, through Jesus\'s death, they realized the weight and truth of His words and that everything happened just as He had said. Because they were inside the council, they were aware of various wrongdoings and understood that they should move in the right direction.

\-\--

\*\*Luke 23:54\*\*

\"It was the day of Preparation, and the Sabbath was beginning.\"

\*\*Luke 23:55\*\*

\"The women who had come with him from Galilee followed and saw the tomb and how his body was laid.\"

\*\*Luke 23:56\*\*

\"Then they returned and prepared spices and ointments. On the Sabbath they rested according to the commandment.\"

\-\--

What we notice here is the absence of the disciples, as mentioned earlier. Whether Joseph\'s courageous actions and the women who watched believed in the resurrection is uncertain. However, in doing what they could, these women were doing the right thing.

I would like to add that although the disciples could not stand rightly before Jesus at this time, they were given another opportunity, and they were able to seize it.

Now, there is something recorded only in Matthew---the attitude of the Sanhedrin.

\-\--

\*\*Matthew 27:62\*\*

\"The next day, that is, after the day of Preparation, the chief priests and the Pharisees gathered before Pilate\"

\*\*Matthew 27:63\*\*

\"and said, \'Sir, we remember how that impostor said while he was still alive, \"After three days I will rise.\"\"

\*\*Matthew 27:64\*\*

\"Therefore order the tomb to be made secure until the third day, lest his disciples go and steal him away and tell the people, \"He has risen from the dead,\" and the last fraud will be worse than the first.\'\"

\*\*Matthew 27:65\*\*

\"Pilate said to them, \'You have a guard of soldiers. Go, make it as secure as you can.\'\"

\*\*Matthew 27:66\*\*

\"So they went and made the tomb secure by sealing the stone and setting a guard.\"

\-\--

Firstly, what\'s interesting here is that since it says \"the next day, that is, after the day of Preparation,\" they are doing these things on the Sabbath. Despite having accused Jesus so vehemently, they are here easily breaking the Sabbath. However, the most serious violation of the Law is undoubtedly in:

\-\--

\*\*Leviticus 19:11\*\*

\"You shall not steal; you shall not deal falsely; you shall not lie to one another.\"

@@@@

\*\*Matthew 27:61\*\*

\"Mary Magdalene and the other Mary were sitting there opposite the tomb.\"

→ This also shows that they were observing the Sabbath. Joseph and the others were hurrying to prepare before the Sabbath.

It is a hidden truth in this scene that those who carefully watched over Jesus\'s burial were keeping the Law, while those who drove Jesus to death were breaking it---a reversal of roles.

This attitude of the Sanhedrin leads to the following passage.

\-\--

\*\*Matthew 28:11\*\*

\"While they were going, behold, some of the guard went into the city and told the chief priests all that had taken place.\"

\*\*Matthew 28:12\*\*

\"And when they had assembled with the elders and taken counsel, they gave a sufficient sum of money to the soldiers\"

\*\*Matthew 28:13\*\*

\"and said, \'Tell people, \"His disciples came by night and stole him away while we were asleep.\"\'\"

\*\*Matthew 28:14\*\*

\"And if this comes to the governor\'s ears, we will satisfy him and keep you out of trouble.\"

\*\*Matthew 28:15\*\*

\"So they took the money and did as they were directed. And this story has been spread among the Jews to this day.\"

\-\--

Looking at this passage, we can understand that such rumors were likely widespread around the early church. It is revealed here that they were fabricated through such means.

Considering what the council achieved with these underhanded methods, it became clear that the resurrection truly happened---that it was a miracle of God. In other words, it was revealed that Jesus is the Christ and the Savior. Because they themselves had sealed the tomb, the fact that the body disappeared from there proved that an impossible event had occurred.

In other words, their actions ended up highlighting the greatness of Jesus\'s resurrection. The Lord will prove everything. Let us entrust ourselves to Him.

Now, what we should ponder today is the hearts of those who buried and watched over Jesus. Did they believe in the resurrection, that decisive miracle? Probably, they had not yet believed in it. If they had believed, they might not have performed such a burial.

However, even so, I think they did everything they could out of their love for Jesus. Joseph\'s actions could be considered dangerous, possibly causing him to lose everything. Yet, he did it.

\*\*What was the result of their actions?\*\*

What did they gain through this burial?

They came to know, more than anyone else, that Jesus had truly died. As they wrapped His body, they could confirm with their own hands that there was no pulse and that His body had lost its warmth.

And when He rose again, they knew better than anyone else that this was an act of God that could not be deceived or fabricated.

Joseph eventually became an evangelist who left behind many legends. That\'s because he believed in Jesus when there was nothing to believe in, moved forward, and was given great things. He witnessed a miracle in a place where there was nothing---knowing better than anyone else that Jesus had truly died.

The testimony given to Joseph and the others has become the greatest evidence in proving that Jesus is the Savior.

Joseph and the others heard Jesus\'s words, examined His teachings deeply, and knew they were true. They saw Him doing what was right---loving and helping people. In essence, that\'s all there was.

In other words, what was spoken and what was done---that alone moved them. All other factors likely told them it would be better not to act. The crucifixion, the voices of the world, the significant financial cost---all these things would have stopped them. Yet, they followed the righteousness in their hearts. They obeyed the convictions given through Jesus about what is right and what is wrong.

As a result, they were given something wonderful: the conviction that Jesus is the true God. They came to know this sooner and more strongly than anyone else.

\*\*We should be like them.\*\*

Do we believe because a miracle happened? Rather, we should believe in Jesus precisely when there is nothing.

Importantly, we always hear the Word and always pray; we know what is right.

So then, what should we do?

It is to serve Jesus. Joseph and the others, even against everything in the world, followed the righteousness in their hearts. Many excuses probably crossed their minds. But by taking the right action at the right time given by God---taking the right step forward---they obtained an incomparable strength of faith.

\*\*How is our faith life now?\*\*

How are the issues in the church?

In various situations, the Lord gives us times and opportunities. These are chances to do what is right before the Lord.

\- \*\*What kind of attitude should we take among people?\*\*

\- \*\*How should we respond to the voices of the world?\*\*

\- \*\*How should we think about the problems in the church?\*\*

\- \*\*What should we consider in places unseen by others?\*\*

In our daily lives, we are always called to make decisions by the Lord.

\*\*Whom will you serve now? Will you serve Me, or will you serve the world?\*\*

When that voice is in your heart, I hope you will not hesitate.

Because if you do not follow that voice, you will not notice the grace. If you always follow the excuses within yourself---staying in the same place---the scenery will never change. We Christians must step forward. The reason is not something in this world; we should move forward by faith.

And it\'s not that we proceed because some miracle happened or because we have some assurance. Even if there seems to be no basis from a human perspective, we should decide to move forward with the Word.

\-\--

In other words, I want you all to face the challenges given to you.

\- \*\*What should I do now for the Lord?\*\*

\- \*\*How should I serve?\*\*

\- \*\*What kind of behavior of mine will convey the righteousness of the Word?\*\*

Each of us holds these things differently in our respective places. However, it is also a voice that resonates in your hearts through the Holy Spirit.

Please listen to that voice. I hope you will respond to that challenge. Like Joseph and the others, please step out of your own realm and head toward it.

And I hope you will feel the wonderful power of the Lord that you can understand only because you took that step forward.

Not because something happened, but because you moved first, I want to strengthen our faith through the signs of Jesus.
