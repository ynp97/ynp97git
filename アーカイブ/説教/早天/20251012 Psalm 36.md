20251012 Psalm 36:5--9 "The Grace That Is Poured Out." 

The word "nature" in Japanese, *shizen*, was created relatively recently as a translation of the English word *nature*, which itself comes from the Latin term meaning "that which is born" or "the natural state."

Now, let us look together at the text of Scripture.

In Psalm 36:5--6, we see that everything---from the heavens to the depths of the sea---is under God's hand. The scope of His dominion is vast.

**Psalm 36:5--6 (ESV)**

5 Your steadfast love, O Lord, extends to the heavens,

 your faithfulness to the clouds.

6 Your righteousness is like the mountains of God;

 your judgments are like the great deep;

 man and beast you save, O Lord.

God rules the whole world with His will and justice, and He judges rightly, acknowledging the righteous as righteous.

Verse 6 says, "You save man and beast." We often think that salvation concerns humans alone, but Scripture reveals that God's saving work extends to all of creation.

**Psalm 36:7 (ESV)**

How precious is your steadfast love, O God!

 The children of mankind take refuge in the shadow of your wings.

Because of this divine nature, we are protected. This is the "grace" given to us by God.

Verses 8--9 describe in greater detail how this grace is poured into our lives:

**Psalm 36:8--9 (ESV)**

8 They feast on the abundance of your house,

 and you give them drink from the river of your delights.

9 For with you is the fountain of life;

 in your light do we see light.

"They feast on the abundance of your house, and you give them drink from the river of your delights."

It is a scene overflowing with joy. Yet the crucial phrase here is "the abundance of *your* house."

This abundance is not what we ourselves imagine abundance to be---it is *God's* abundance.

Why is it different from what we call abundance? Because it relates to the very origin of human sin.

As seen in Genesis, when humankind rebelled against God, He foresaw that we would become opposed to nature. We were created to live in unity with it, yet we turned away.

But if we once again "take refuge in the shadow of His wings," trusting in God as our Savior, our condition changes. Through Christ, we are redeemed from sin and set free from its dominion.

Thus, we are transformed from those who oppose creation into those who find joy in living together with it.

And what is given to such a life? Verse 9 speaks of "the fountain of life."

From that fountain flows living water---the water of life that fills us with strength and vitality.

That life-giving energy has a clear purpose: it empowers us to walk toward the light of Christ.

When our purpose becomes clear and we are given strength to live toward it,

we experience the most blessed condition a human being can know---to live as truly human.

And what is that purpose? It is to live in *love*.

To live in love means to protect what we hold dear, and to see the entire world as precious---overflowing with care wherever we are.

To coexist with nature, to truly love creation---there lies our purpose.

And as we walk in that purpose, God gives us the strength to love.

To love family, to love friends, to love all people, and likewise to love nature and the environment---

this joy of loving is the light God shows us. It is the very meaning of our existence.

In our daily lives, things often do not go as we expect.

Even today, as we enjoy a barbecue, we may worry, "What if it rains?"

But if we face nature rightly, even the rain is a blessing.

A barbecue held at church will surely be full of joy.

We tend to see whatever does not go our way as hardship,

but in truth, every such event too lies within God's hand.

Whether it rains, grows hot, or turns cold---

if we can receive all these natural occurrences and find joy within them,

life itself overflows with energy and delight.

Some of us may be burdened today with worries or sorrows.

Yet we are those who believe in Jesus Christ.

The God who governs all creation knows each of us personally

and has already prepared everything we truly need.

Let us return to our *true natural state*---

that is, to our rightful condition as people freed from sin---

through faith in Jesus Christ.

And let us live each moment, each breath of our lives,

filled with the living water of life, rejoicing in God's grace,

living energetically and with hope.

As we celebrate our 35th anniversary,

may we continue to be such a church---overflowing with grace poured out from God.
